About IIIT - Sri City

Indian Institute of Information Technology Chittoor at Sri City, known as IIITS, is an institute of national importance and a pinnacle of technology education standards in India. The Institute is governed by distinguished academicians, industry leaders and senior government officials, referred to as the Board of Governors. Additionally, IIIT Hyderabad and IIT Hyderabad are the mentor institutions for IIIT Sri City. In fact, The Institute follows a similar curriculum and pedagogy as IIIT Hyderabad.

IIITS was established under the Government of India’s Initiative to set up 20 Institutes across India, focused on Information Technology. The Institute was setup by the Government of India – MHRD, Government of Andhra Pradesh and Industry Partners (Sri City Foundation) as a Not-for-Profit Public-Private-Partnership. The Institute was funded 50 percent by the Government of India, 35 percent by the Government of Andhra Pradesh and 15 percent by the Industry Partners.

Curriculam

IIITS follows a similar curriculum as IIIT-Hyderabad and leading American and European Universities. Unlike regular Engineering Institutions which follow class-room based learning and examination based grading, we focus on problem-solving, real-world projects and group education at IIITS. The Institute provides its students the opportunity to select courses and projects of their Choice. The Institute also allows provides its students the opportunity to participate in variety of research, technology development and community Programs. Overall, The Institute curriculum is modern, competitive and demands hard-work from each Student. Note that admission to Maters and PHD Programs is done twice a year during the Monsoon and Spring Semesters.

Undergraduate Program

Indian Institute of Information Technology at Sri City offers the following Undergraduate Programs, which are focused on Information Technology:

Bachelor in Technology with Major in Computer Science and Engineering (200 Seats)

Bachelor in Technology with Major in Electronics and Communication Engineering (70 Seats)
